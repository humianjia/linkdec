Content Policy

How the public site handles editorial summaries, third-party game pages, and removal requests.

Last updated: May 10, 2026

1. Editorial Scope

Traffic Games Online publishes a limited public set of editorial pages and selected browser-playable pages related to traffic, driving, parking, and route-management game themes.

2. What We Add

When a page is intentionally public, we aim to add original page structure such as a summary, control notes, troubleshooting guidance, category context, or other editorial information that helps readers understand the page before launching gameplay.

3. Third-Party And Embedded Content

Some playable experiences may rely on third-party content, assets, or embedded technology. Where that is the case, the underlying game rights, branding, and platform rules may belong to their respective owners.

4. Review And Cleanup Standards

  • We keep the public sitemap intentionally smaller than the full archive while pages are being refreshed
  • Legacy template pages may remain accessible for maintenance but are not intended to be part of the main public review set
  • Pages that do not meet the current public standard can be reduced in visibility, updated, or retired

5. Copyright And Attribution Requests

If you believe a public page includes material that should be credited, corrected, limited, or removed, email business@trafficgames.online with the page URL, the material at issue, and the basis for your request.

6. Content Removal Requests

We review good-faith requests involving copyright, branding, ownership, or other legitimate content concerns. Please include enough detail for us to identify the page and understand the requested action. We may ask for additional information before making a change.

7. Response Expectations

We aim to review straightforward content or ownership requests within 2 business days, though complex questions involving third-party providers may take longer.
